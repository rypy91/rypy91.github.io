About me

I am a geophysicist who seeks to better understand the role of fluids in seismicity. I build large numerical models to investigate the interaction between fluids and faults within the Earth. I characterize fault stability by resolving the spatial and temporal distribution of stress and pore pressure caused by natural and anthropogenic induced seismicity.

My work links information across different scales of observation. It spans single point field-based observations up to large-scale models of regional fluid perturbations. I combine physical-based models with statistics and machine/deep learning techniques to better understand process-oriented questions.

I am broadly interested in numerical modeling, fluid-effects on faults, induced seismicity, seismic hazard and mitigation, machine/deep learning, carbon sequestration, hydrology, and hydrogeodesy.

Research

Major southern San Andreas earthquakes modulated by lake-filling events

New geologic and numerical model results provide evidence that ancient Lake Cahuilla was sufficient for triggering past earthquakes on the Southern San Andreas Fault. PDF

Mitigation and optimization of induced seismicity using physics-based forecasting (in prep.)

We couple a numerical model and statistical techniques to better mitigate the seismic hazard associated with wastewater injection.

Deciphering earthquake triggering mechanisms with a fully coupled poroelastic model and machine learning analysis... (in prep.)

We explore earthquake triggering mechanisms by combining a physical model, machine learning methods, and temporal clustering techniques.

Smoke and Fire Detection with YOLOv2

For a final project we built a deep learning YOLOv2 CNN for the use in real-time wildfire detection. Final Report.

fSCA Detection

I built a parsimonious algorithm to detect fractional snow cover area (fSCA) for hyperspectral images. MSc Thesis.
